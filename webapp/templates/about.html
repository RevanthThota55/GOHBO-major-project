What is MedVision AI?

MedVision AI is an advanced medical imaging system that uses GOHBO-optimized ResNet-18 architectures to classify medical images across multiple modalities - brain MRI scans, chest X-rays, and colorectal histopathology.

Unlike traditional black-box models, our system provides visual explanations through Grad-CAM heatmaps and uncertainty quantification via Monte Carlo Dropout, giving clinicians the confidence and transparency needed for medical decision support.

Each model is trained on thousands of medical images and achieves publication-grade accuracy while maintaining real-time inference speeds suitable for clinical deployment.

Classification Capabilities

Our models identify fourteen distinct medical conditions across three imaging modalities

Glioma Tumor

Malignant tumors arising from glial cells. Requires immediate medical attention and aggressive treatment protocols.

Meningioma Tumor

Typically benign tumors from protective meninges layers. Often slow-growing with good prognosis when treated early.

No Tumor / Normal

Healthy scan with no detectable abnormal growths, masses, or pathological findings. Normal anatomical structures visible.

Pituitary Tumor

Tumors in the pituitary gland affecting hormone production. Can impact multiple body systems and require specialized care.

Pneumonia

Infection causing inflammation in lung air sacs. Detected through opacity or consolidation patterns visible in chest X-rays.

Normal Lungs

Clear lung fields with no signs of infiltrates, consolidation, or effusion. Normal cardiac silhouette and thoracic structures.

Tumor Tissue

Colorectal adenocarcinoma epithelium. Malignant tissue requiring immediate clinical attention and treatment planning.

Stroma

Cancer-associated stroma tissue. Connective tissue surrounding tumor cells that plays a key role in tumor microenvironment.

Complex Stroma

Mixed tissue with complex stromal patterns containing both immune and structural components requiring careful analysis.

Lymphocytes

Immune cell infiltration. High lymphocyte presence may indicate immune response to tumor and can influence treatment strategy.

Debris

Cellular debris and necrotic tissue. May indicate areas of tissue damage or tumor necrosis in the specimen.

Mucosa

Normal mucosal glands of the colon. Healthy intestinal lining tissue with normal glandular architecture.

Adipose

Fatty tissue. Normal adipose cells found in the submucosal and serosal layers of the colorectal wall.

Empty Background

Empty or background regions of the histopathology slide with no significant tissue content present.

Why GOHBO?

Traditional hyperparameter optimization methods often get stuck in local optima or require extensive computational resources. GOHBO solves both problems.

By combining the exploration capabilities of Grey Wolf Optimizer with the exploitation efficiency of Heap-Based structures and the diversity maintenance of Orthogonal Learning, GOHBO finds optimal hyperparameters faster than traditional methods.

The result? Models that train smarter, not harder - achieving higher accuracy with fewer training epochs and better generalization to unseen data.
